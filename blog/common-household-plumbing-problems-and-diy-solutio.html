Common Household Plumbing Problems and DIY Solutions

Understanding common plumbing issues and knowing when to DIY or call a professional can save you time and money. Here's our comprehensive guide to household plumbing problems and their solutions.

1. Dripping Faucets

A dripping faucet isn't just annoying – it can waste thousands of liters of water annually. The most common cause is worn-out washers or O-rings. To fix:

  • Turn off the water supply
  • Disassemble the faucet handle
  • Replace the washer or O-ring
  • Reassemble carefully

2. Clogged Drains

Slow or clogged drains are among the most common plumbing issues. Before reaching for harsh chemicals, try these steps:

  • Use a plunger specifically designed for the fixture
  • Try a mixture of baking soda and vinegar
  • Use a plumber's snake for stubborn clogs
  • Clean the P-trap under sinks

3. Running Toilets

A running toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water daily. Common causes include:

  • Faulty flapper valve
  • Incorrect float height
  • Damaged fill valve
  • Loose chain

4. Low Water Pressure

Low water pressure can result from several issues:

  • Clogged aerators
  • Leaking pipes
  • Water main problems
  • Mineral buildup in pipes

When to Call a Professional

While many plumbing issues can be resolved with DIY solutions, some situations require professional attention:

  • Frozen or burst pipes
  • Sewer system backups
  • Water heater issues
  • Major pipe leaks
  • Complex installations or repairs

Preventive Maintenance Tips

Regular maintenance can prevent many common plumbing problems:

  • Regularly inspect visible pipes for leaks
  • Don't pour grease down drains
  • Use drain strainers
  • Schedule annual professional inspections
  • Know where your main water shut-off valve is located
